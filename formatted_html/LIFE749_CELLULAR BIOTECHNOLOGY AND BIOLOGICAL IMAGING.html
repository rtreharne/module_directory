Blurb
Overview
This module introduces students to a variety of methods in mammalian cell biotechnology as well as imaging technologies that range from the microscopic scale to cellular and organ imaging in vivo in animal models of disease. The module content will cover various cell culture methods specific for defined cell types and/or experimental aims (e.g. 2D and 3D/organoid cultures) and convey knowledge in a diversity of cell analysis techniques, including cytometry, microscopy and spectrometry applications. Various approaches for the genetic manipulation of cells and their suitability for different purposes will be presented. The use of reporter genes for various types of imaging will be explained, and imaging technologies for cell analysis on the microscopic level as well as for cell imaging and functional analysis in animal models of disease, e.g. via MRI or bioluminescence, will be covered. The module will mainly focus on examples of stem cell biotechnology in regenerative medicine and tumour cell metabolism in oncology research. Ethical aspects and appropriate scientific conduct will be discussed in these contexts as well. The module will be taught through a combination of lectures, workshops and practical exercises.
Educational Aims
To enhance the core skills acquired , including both scientific (presentational and communication) and employability skills, and to provide advice on careers and career development in imaging technologies for cell analysis on the microscopic level as well as for cell imaging and functional analysis in animal models.

To enable students to evaluate the latest scientific literature and technologies in imaging technologies for cell analysis on the microscopic level as well as for cell imaging and functional analysis in animal models of disease and topical issues of particular concern to biotechnologist.

To enhance problem solving skills by data analysis exercises in relation to experimental methods in imaging and cell biotechnology, and develop a deeper understanding of topical issues in the subject.
Marketing Module Synopsis
Modern biotechnology and bioimaging applies novel tools and approaches to address today's global challenges.
You will learn a variety of methods in mammalian cell biotechnology as well as imaging technologies that range from the microscopic scale to cellular and organ imaging in vivo. You will develop knowledge of a diversity of cell analysis techniques. Furthermore, the use of reporter genes for various types of imaging will be explained, including imaging technologies for cell analysis on the microscopic level as well as for cell imaging and functional analysis in animal models of disease.

The lectures will convey basic knowledge and include examples of applications from actual research publications, or the lecturer’s own research work, in equal measure. The students will have learning tutorials on critical appraisal of literature. There will also be a practical workshop on contemporary microscopy.
The module will be taught through a combination of lectures, workshops and practical exercises. There are two written assessments in this module.
Outline Syllabus
Culture techniques, genetic modification and imaging of cells:
a. Genetic manipulation of mammalian cells.
1. CRISPR/Cas
2. Lentivirus vectors, homologous recombination in mouse ESCs.

b. Fluorescence microscopy and labels, reporter genes in genetic modification and for imaging.
c. Basic elements of flow cytometry & mass cytometry.
d. 3D cell culture and 3D imaging
1. Organoids and mouse tumour models.
2. Tumour spheroids.
e. Elements of image analysis:

Stem cells in regenerative medicine and in cancer:
a. An introduction to Stem cells in regenerative medicine and in cancer and applications:
1. embryonic stem cells,
2. stem cells in adult tissues.
3. Biomaterials in (stem) cell culture and tissue engineering

b. Ethics and scientific conduct in research
1. Controversial views regarding stem cells
2. Scientific conduct in research.
Imaging of cells in animal models and spectroscopic imaging:
a. In vivo imaging modalities,
b. Tumour and stem cell tracking in vivo,
c. Imaging of physiological functions in animals, including substrates, contrast reagents and nanomaterials for imaging.
Teaching and Learning Methods
Scheduled Lectures: Research focus lectures will examine areas of current interest in more detail or employability issues and opportunities related to the topic area.
Attendance Recorded: yes
Workshops: these are intended to give students the digital fluency required to build and analyse experiments, and present research findings. Discussion on the understanding of Ethics and scientific conduct in research will also be part of the workshops.
Attendance Recorded: yes

The student directed learning element of the module will involve directed reading, completion of workbooks, accessing online resources, searching the literature and critically appraising articles. This will require adaptability & organisational skills.

Curriculum 2021 and the Liverpool Hallmarks are at the centre of this module. Digital fluency is practiced by using online platforms. Research-connected teaching is embedded in all aspects of teaching as the module is taught by experts in their field. Group discussion will be part of the workshops fostering critical thinking. This module contributes to an MSc in (MSc in Biotechnology//Cancer Biology and Therapy), which is specifically designed to support students for future training in Life Sciences and provide ample scope for employability.
The teaching approach was designed to promote students’ active learning by taking part in workshop discussions. Communication skill will also be exercised in the workshop discussion and assessment (Video presentation, conference style).

“Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for teaching may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.”
Assessment Strategy
The module will be assessed via two coursework assessments. The assessments are designed to be authentic to the research experience.
The first assessment consists of an online presentation in a format of a video presentation, conference style, based on lecture/tutorial material. Video presentation is designed to develop confidence, preparing students for a professional environment, and confidence via development of communication skills (C2021). This assessment will contribute to 30% of the module mark.

The second assessment, students will have to present a written report on a research plan. Work places expect employees to be able to tackle problems and provide solutions. The design of a research plan should help students to develop such skills (C2021). This assessment will contribute to 70% of the module mark.

All assessments will be provided with feedback. Pass mark for all assessments is 50%. Due to the nature of the first assessment, this is a non-anomymous assessment. The final report is an anonymous assessment.

Reassessment strategy: Resit questions will have the same format as the original assessment but will focus on a different topic on the syllabus and will take place in the reassessment window and will be capped at 50%. Resits for the first assessment will be graded by data analysis and writing a small report.

Standard UoL penalty applies for late submission of all assessments.

Learning materials will be available to students via the University Virtual Learning environment.

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for assessment may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.

Blurb
Overview
This module introduces students to a variety of methods in mammalian cell biotechnology as well as imaging technologies that range from the microscopic scale to cellular and organ imaging in vivo in animal models of disease. The module content will cover various cell culture methods specific for defined cell types and/or experimental aims (e.g. 2D and 3D/organoid cultures) and convey knowledge in a diversity of cell analysis techniques, including cytometry, microscopy and spectrometry applications. Various approaches for the genetic manipulation of cells and their suitability for different purposes will be presented. The use of reporter genes for various types of imaging will be explained, and imaging technologies for cell analysis on the microscopic level as well as for cell imaging and functional analysis in animal models of disease, e.g. via MRI or bioluminescence, will be covered. The module will mainly focus on examples of stem cell biotechnology in regenerative medicine and tumour cell metabolism in oncology research. Ethical aspects and appropriate scientific conduct will be discussed in these contexts as well. The module will be taught through a combination of lectures, workshops and practical exercises.
Educational Aims
To enhance the core skills acquired , including both scientific (presentational and communication) and employability skills, and to provide advice on careers and career development in imaging technologies for cell analysis on the microscopic level as well as for cell imaging and functional analysis in animal models.

To enable students to evaluate the latest scientific literature and technologies in imaging technologies for cell analysis on the microscopic level as well as for cell imaging and functional analysis in animal models of disease and topical issues of particular concern to biotechnologist.

To enhance problem solving skills by data analysis exercises in relation to experimental methods in imaging and cell biotechnology, and develop a deeper understanding of topical issues in the subject.
Marketing Module Synopsis
Modern biotechnology and bioimaging applies novel tools and approaches to address today's global challenges.
You will learn a variety of methods in mammalian cell biotechnology as well as imaging technologies that range from the microscopic scale to cellular and organ imaging in vivo. You will develop knowledge of a diversity of cell analysis techniques. Furthermore, the use of reporter genes for various types of imaging will be explained, including imaging technologies for cell analysis on the microscopic level as well as for cell imaging and functional analysis in animal models of disease.

The lectures will convey basic knowledge and include examples of applications from actual research publications, or the lecturer’s own research work, in equal measure. The students will have learning tutorials on critical appraisal of literature. There will also be a practical workshop on contemporary microscopy.
The module will be taught through a combination of lectures, workshops and practical exercises. There are two written assessments in this module.
Outline Syllabus
Culture techniques, genetic modification and imaging of cells:
a. Genetic manipulation of mammalian cells.
1. CRISPR/Cas
2. Lentivirus vectors, homologous recombination in mouse ESCs.

b. Fluorescence microscopy and labels, reporter genes in genetic modification and for imaging.
c. Basic elements of flow cytometry & mass cytometry.
d. 3D cell culture and 3D imaging
1. Organoids and mouse tumour models.
2. Tumour spheroids.
e. Elements of image analysis:

Stem cells in regenerative medicine and in cancer:
a. An introduction to Stem cells in regenerative medicine and in cancer and applications:
1. embryonic stem cells,
2. stem cells in adult tissues.
3. Biomaterials in (stem) cell culture and tissue engineering

b. Ethics and scientific conduct in research
1. Controversial views regarding stem cells
2. Scientific conduct in research.
Imaging of cells in animal models and spectroscopic imaging:
a. In vivo imaging modalities,
b. Tumour and stem cell tracking in vivo,
c. Imaging of physiological functions in animals, including substrates, contrast reagents and nanomaterials for imaging.
Teaching and Learning Methods
Scheduled Lectures: Research focus lectures will examine areas of current interest in more detail or employability issues and opportunities related to the topic area.
Attendance Recorded: yes
Workshops: these are intended to give students the digital fluency required to build and analyse experiments, and present research findings. Discussion on the understanding of Ethics and scientific conduct in research will also be part of the workshops.
Attendance Recorded: yes

The student directed learning element of the module will involve directed reading, completion of workbooks, accessing online resources, searching the literature and critically appraising articles. This will require adaptability & organisational skills.

Curriculum 2021 and the Liverpool Hallmarks are at the centre of this module. Digital fluency is practiced by using online platforms. Research-connected teaching is embedded in all aspects of teaching as the module is taught by experts in their field. Group discussion will be part of the workshops fostering critical thinking. This module contributes to an MSc in (MSc in Biotechnology//Cancer Biology and Therapy), which is specifically designed to support students for future training in Life Sciences and provide ample scope for employability.
The teaching approach was designed to promote students’ active learning by taking part in workshop discussions. Communication skill will also be exercised in the workshop discussion and assessment (Video presentation, conference style).

“Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for teaching may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.”
Assessment Strategy
The module will be assessed via two coursework assessments. The assessments are designed to be authentic to the research experience.
The first assessment consists of an online presentation in a format of a video presentation, conference style, based on lecture/tutorial material. Video presentation is designed to develop confidence, preparing students for a professional environment, and confidence via development of communication skills (C2021). This assessment will contribute to 30% of the module mark.

The second assessment, students will have to present a written report on a research plan. Work places expect employees to be able to tackle problems and provide solutions. The design of a research plan should help students to develop such skills (C2021). This assessment will contribute to 70% of the module mark.

All assessments will be provided with feedback. Pass mark for all assessments is 50%. Due to the nature of the first assessment, this is a non-anomymous assessment. The final report is an anonymous assessment.

Reassessment strategy: Resit questions will have the same format as the original assessment but will focus on a different topic on the syllabus and will take place in the reassessment window and will be capped at 50%. Resits for the first assessment will be graded by data analysis and writing a small report.

Standard UoL penalty applies for late submission of all assessments.

Learning materials will be available to students via the University Virtual Learning environment.

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for assessment may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.
