Blurb
Overview
This module will build on previous skills modules, developing skills in critical thinking, data/literature interpretation and analysis, scientific writing, communication, research skills and teamwork. The module consists of lectures, workshops and tutorials.

Students will be given two summative assessments over the course of the module. 1) A piece of science communication writing 2) A critical analysis of a piece of research in biochemistry.
Educational Aims
To develop the students' science presentation skills in various formats and to various target audiences;

To enhance the students' ability to search, identify, apply, critically evaluate, and interpret biochemical knowledge to solve complex problems;

To provide advice, inspiration and guidance for career development and employability.​
Marketing Module Synopsis
This module aims to provide a set of communication and critical reflection skills in the context of biochemistry. These skills will help students enhance the execution and presentation of research projects, prepare them for Level 6 assessments and maximise their employment and personal development opportunities. The module is taught though seminars and tutorials, but has a strong emphasis on independent learning. Learning material is provided through VITAL. The module is assessed by continuous assessment.
Outline Syllabus
Critical Thinking

Students on this module will be introduced to the key principles of critical thinking and will apply their skills to perform critical analyses of the scientific literature and produce a peer review for an article in the area of biochemistry.

Science Communication

The public perception of science is of increasing concern to scientists, policymakers, and educators. Students will learn about the modern challenges in science communication and the importance of good science communication in the area of biochemistry. These skills will be put into practice in a written piece and a poster presentation.

Research Skills

Students will develop their research skills from previous modules and apply them to analyse literature in the area of biochemistry.
Teaching and Learning Methods
The critical analysis assessment will be supported by an introductory lecture and 4 mid-sized group sessions facilitated by an academic. Ahead of these sessions, students will be assigned reading which will form the basis of the discussion. The group will work to produce a collaboratively written document which will then serve as support material for the final assessment. Each session is connected to a research paper/area (selected by the academic) thus making the sessions "research-connected" as well as giving the students an authentic experience to actively train their critical analysis skills on cutting edge research.

The scientific writing assessment will be supported by workshops to aid the students in developing their writing skills as well as giving them an understanding of the wider significance of good science communication. The aim of these sessions is to give the students confidence in their ability to communicate scientific complexities to non-specialist audiences and an understanding of how good science communication is an important aspect of global citizenship. There will be a session set aside for students to receive formative feedback to assist in this assignment.
Across the module there will be a mix of asynchronous and synchronous content. 1hr per week of teaching will be delivered synchronously online and 1 hr per week will be delivered in-person in socially distanced groups

“Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for teaching may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.”
Assessment Strategy
Assessment on this module is split into 2 parts. Each part aims to meet the 2021 requirement of authenticity as described below.

1) Scientific writing. Based on major themes in current research, students will produce a piece of scientific writing for non-specialist audiences.
2) Critical Analysis. Students will be assessed on their ability to critically evaluate scientific literature by producing a peer review/grant proposal.

Rationale

Across the various programmes within Life Sciences, there is significant diversity amongst the third year advanced skills modules in terms of both student and staff workload. Because of this, programme directors and module leaders were brought together for a series of meetings to discuss a solution. Student feedback and performance on the existing modules was taken into account and the various components of each module were evaluated.

Following this review of the school's provision, it was decided that several programmes should be brought together onto similar module structures. This module is one of seven modules being brought onto this structure.

The previous 301 module contained 4 assessments (A data analysis exercise, a poster presentation, production of a video and an article critique). The new proposed structure will have 3 coursework assessments in line with school policy. The proposed assessments consist of two written pieces (40% each) and a poster presentation (20%).

Advice on the best teaching methods to employ to deliver the content in support of each assessment was sought from CIE.

Blurb
Overview
This module will build on previous skills modules, developing skills in critical thinking, data/literature interpretation and analysis, scientific writing, communication, research skills and teamwork. The module consists of lectures, workshops and tutorials.

Students will be given two summative assessments over the course of the module. 1) A piece of science communication writing 2) A critical analysis of a piece of research in biochemistry.
Educational Aims
To develop the students' science presentation skills in various formats and to various target audiences;

To enhance the students' ability to search, identify, apply, critically evaluate, and interpret biochemical knowledge to solve complex problems;

To provide advice, inspiration and guidance for career development and employability.​
Marketing Module Synopsis
This module aims to provide a set of communication and critical reflection skills in the context of biochemistry. These skills will help students enhance the execution and presentation of research projects, prepare them for Level 6 assessments and maximise their employment and personal development opportunities. The module is taught though seminars and tutorials, but has a strong emphasis on independent learning. Learning material is provided through VITAL. The module is assessed by continuous assessment.
Outline Syllabus
Critical Thinking

Students on this module will be introduced to the key principles of critical thinking and will apply their skills to perform critical analyses of the scientific literature and produce a peer review for an article in the area of biochemistry.

Science Communication

The public perception of science is of increasing concern to scientists, policymakers, and educators. Students will learn about the modern challenges in science communication and the importance of good science communication in the area of biochemistry. These skills will be put into practice in a written piece and a poster presentation.

Research Skills

Students will develop their research skills from previous modules and apply them to analyse literature in the area of biochemistry.
Teaching and Learning Methods
The critical analysis assessment will be supported by an introductory lecture and 4 mid-sized group sessions facilitated by an academic. Ahead of these sessions, students will be assigned reading which will form the basis of the discussion. The group will work to produce a collaboratively written document which will then serve as support material for the final assessment. Each session is connected to a research paper/area (selected by the academic) thus making the sessions "research-connected" as well as giving the students an authentic experience to actively train their critical analysis skills on cutting edge research.

The scientific writing assessment will be supported by workshops to aid the students in developing their writing skills as well as giving them an understanding of the wider significance of good science communication. The aim of these sessions is to give the students confidence in their ability to communicate scientific complexities to non-specialist audiences and an understanding of how good science communication is an important aspect of global citizenship. There will be a session set aside for students to receive formative feedback to assist in this assignment.
Across the module there will be a mix of asynchronous and synchronous content. 1hr per week of teaching will be delivered synchronously online and 1 hr per week will be delivered in-person in socially distanced groups

“Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for teaching may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.”
Assessment Strategy
Assessment on this module is split into 2 parts. Each part aims to meet the 2021 requirement of authenticity as described below.

1) Scientific writing. Based on major themes in current research, students will produce a piece of scientific writing for non-specialist audiences.
2) Critical Analysis. Students will be assessed on their ability to critically evaluate scientific literature by producing a peer review/grant proposal.

Rationale

Across the various programmes within Life Sciences, there is significant diversity amongst the third year advanced skills modules in terms of both student and staff workload. Because of this, programme directors and module leaders were brought together for a series of meetings to discuss a solution. Student feedback and performance on the existing modules was taken into account and the various components of each module were evaluated.

Following this review of the school's provision, it was decided that several programmes should be brought together onto similar module structures. This module is one of seven modules being brought onto this structure.

The previous 301 module contained 4 assessments (A data analysis exercise, a poster presentation, production of a video and an article critique). The new proposed structure will have 3 coursework assessments in line with school policy. The proposed assessments consist of two written pieces (40% each) and a poster presentation (20%).

Advice on the best teaching methods to employ to deliver the content in support of each assessment was sought from CIE.
