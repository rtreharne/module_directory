Educational Aims
To introduce students to authentic research experiences, allowing them to pursue active enquiry and to develop research skills which will be transferable to academic and other professional settings.

To offer structured learning activities and to support students to choose and plan their learning under close supervision.
Marketing Module Synopsis
This module is for international exchange students. The module provides students with the opportunity to participate in a research project and work in partnership directly with a member of academic staff in the School of Life Sciences.

Students will be supervised by an experienced member of academic staff. Each student will be part of an active research environment, where they will connect with prominent researchers and contribute to live research. They will either perform a short standalone project within an existing research team or will take part in a pre-existing project, within which the student will have clearly defined goals.
Outline Syllabus
Students will carry out a 6 week research project under the supervision of a member of UoL staff associated with the School of Life Sciences.

Students will join an existing research team and will either perform a short standalone project, or take part in a pre-existing project within which the student will have clearly defined goals.

Specific project descriptions will be provided to students in advance of selecting the module. Students will indicate which project(s) they wish to apply to take part in.
Teaching and Learning Methods
Weekly Supervision Meetings: These are regular one to one meetings with the supervisor to discuss project and assignment planning, and progress towards meeting the learning outcomes

Project Work - This refers to available time for project work including laboratory work, data analysis, independent reading and write up
Assessment Strategy
The module will be assessed via a poster presentation at a multidisciplinary mini-conference and a portfolio consisting of an overview of the research work completed and a reflection on the students learning and professional development.

Notes
Rationale: This module, for incoming exchange students, provides an opportunity for international students to participate in a research project linked to the active research of UoL academic staff. Students will be able to select this module as a free-standing credit bearing module during the summer vacation months. Credit obtained will be transferable back to the student's home institution.

The scheme is run by the University Study Abroad team in partnership with academic departments. This module will run alongside modules already offered by other schools including Psychology (PSYC001) in this faculty. It is not a new module. It is the use of a pre-existing module in a new School. All summer project students across the university will carry out their projects at the same time each year, coordinated by the study abroad team. There is demand for this type of international experience, as articulated to members of the Study abroad team by our international partners. UoL students will receive opportunities abroad in return.

Student selection: Students apply for this module/international experience via the University Study Abroad Team.

Notes: This is a new Life Sciences module but is based on an approved template provided by the study abroad team that has been approved by UAP and is already in use in other UoL Schools (eg PSYC001). Aims, outcomes and assessments are necessarily the same as those already used in other schools as the modules are run together, coordinated by the study abroad team.
