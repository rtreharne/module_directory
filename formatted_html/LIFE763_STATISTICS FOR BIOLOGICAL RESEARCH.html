Blurb
Overview
This module aims to train biology students in statistical techniques for the analysis and interpretation of data. Students will gain research-level, critical understanding of the necessary theory, and will put this into practice by using statistical software and programming to analyse biological data. The module aims to provide a guide to the statistics that students will need to complete their MSc / MRes. research project. Specifically, it is designed for postgraduate students who have not completed an undergraduate statistics module. The module is taught via directed reading, lectures, and tutorial workshops, and is assessed via SAQ examination, coursework and an individual poster.
Educational Aims
To introduce and advance knowledge of a range of statistical tests that are commonly used in the Biosciences;

To give experience in the implementation of these tests using statistical and programming software;

To enable students to learn how to interpret and analyse the output of these tests.
Marketing Module Synopsis
Successful research in the biological sciences inevitably depends on the power that statistical inference provides for hypothesis testing. Knowing which test to use and when is the key to success.

This module aims to train biology students in statistical techniques for the analysis and interpretation of data. Students will gain research-level, critical understanding of the necessary theory, and will put this into practice by using statistical software and programming to analyse biological data. The module aims to provide a guide to the statistics that students will need to complete their MSc / MRes. research project. Specifically, it is designed for postgraduate students who have not completed an undergraduate statistics module, such as the University of Liverpool's LIFE223 (QS section). The module is taught via directed reading, lectures, and tutorial workshops, and is assessed via SAQ examination, coursework and an individual poster.
Outline Syllabus
Each component of the syllabus will comprise directed reading, followed by a lecture plus a complementary workshop in which students complete formative assessments. These summative assessments build on these formative assessments for both the poster and further in-course assessments.

The course will cover the following topics in the computer workshops:

Introduction and descriptive statistics;

data visualisation;

t tests and hypothesis testing;

ANOVA;

Chi -Squared testing;

Linear Regression;

Non-Linear Regression.
Teaching and Learning Methods
The teaching methods below were designed with Curriculum 2021 Hallmarks and Attributes in mind. Examples given in the online videos demonstrate research-connected teaching as they are created using datasets from active researchers. Active learning is encouraged through the practical computer workshops which allow students to explore and work through the topic material at their own pace. Through the practical workshops, students are building up confidence in their skills and becoming more digitally fluent.

Teaching Method 1 - Videos
Description: Short videos covering each topic are provided on the VLE
Attendance Recorded: No
Notes: These short online videos cover both the underlying theory behind each statistical test, an example of that test in action and then a worked-through example in the statistical software.

Unscheduled Directed Student Hours (time spent away from the timetabled sessions but directed by the teaching staff): 20

Teaching Method 2 - Practical
Description: Tutorial workshops to supplement learning.
Attendance Recorded: Yes
Notes: After each topic is covered in the online videos, a guided worksheet for the corresponding workshop will be released. Students are expected to begin reading and working through the exercises on the worksheet in preparation for the workshop. This work is done on computer which allows for students to have flexibility about where and when the work is done and the worksheets are designed to enable this option. At the end of the workshops are a number of do-it-yourself questions where students have to put their knowledge into practice. The following week, the DIY question answers are released as a walk-through video.
Unscheduled Directed Student Hours (time spent away from the timetabled sessions but directed by the teaching staff): 66

Additional drop-in sessions for 1:1 general and poster assessment support are available as well throughout the course.

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for teaching may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.
Assessment Strategy
The assessment strategy was devised with Curriculum 2021 Hallmarks and Attributes in mind. The assessments detailed below are examples of authentic assessment, such as presenting scientific research at a poster event and research-connected teaching, such as the real-world scientific datasets utilised for the poster assessment. Furthermore, the assessments below build confidence and digital literacy skills in our Masters students to enhance their employability following the course.

Assessment 1 is an SAQ exam. This exam takes place at the end of the course during the January exam period. There is a resit opportunity available for the exam, which is capped at 50%.

Assessment 2: Poster presentation. This will be an individual poster assignment which will be take place at a conference style event (alongside LIFE223 students) in week 12. There is a resit opportunity available for the poster, which is capped at 50%.

Assessment 3: One supplementary pieces of analysis and report writing. In line with the new assessment strategy, this assessment takes place in cycle 1 of the course. This assessment builds upon the material covered in computer tutorials. There is a resit opportunity for the coursework component, which is capped at 50%.

Feedback will be provided to all assessments. Pass mark for all assessments is 50%. Standard UoL penalty applies for late submission.

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for assessment may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.

Blurb
Overview
This module aims to train biology students in statistical techniques for the analysis and interpretation of data. Students will gain research-level, critical understanding of the necessary theory, and will put this into practice by using statistical software and programming to analyse biological data. The module aims to provide a guide to the statistics that students will need to complete their MSc / MRes. research project. Specifically, it is designed for postgraduate students who have not completed an undergraduate statistics module. The module is taught via directed reading, lectures, and tutorial workshops, and is assessed via SAQ examination, coursework and an individual poster.
Educational Aims
To introduce and advance knowledge of a range of statistical tests that are commonly used in the Biosciences;

To give experience in the implementation of these tests using statistical and programming software;

To enable students to learn how to interpret and analyse the output of these tests.
Marketing Module Synopsis
Successful research in the biological sciences inevitably depends on the power that statistical inference provides for hypothesis testing. Knowing which test to use and when is the key to success.

This module aims to train biology students in statistical techniques for the analysis and interpretation of data. Students will gain research-level, critical understanding of the necessary theory, and will put this into practice by using statistical software and programming to analyse biological data. The module aims to provide a guide to the statistics that students will need to complete their MSc / MRes. research project. Specifically, it is designed for postgraduate students who have not completed an undergraduate statistics module, such as the University of Liverpool's LIFE223 (QS section). The module is taught via directed reading, lectures, and tutorial workshops, and is assessed via SAQ examination, coursework and an individual poster.
Outline Syllabus
Each component of the syllabus will comprise directed reading, followed by a lecture plus a complementary workshop in which students complete formative assessments. These summative assessments build on these formative assessments for both the poster and further in-course assessments.

The course will cover the following topics in the computer workshops:

Introduction and descriptive statistics;

data visualisation;

t tests and hypothesis testing;

ANOVA;

Chi -Squared testing;

Linear Regression;

Non-Linear Regression.
Teaching and Learning Methods
The teaching methods below were designed with Curriculum 2021 Hallmarks and Attributes in mind. Examples given in the online videos demonstrate research-connected teaching as they are created using datasets from active researchers. Active learning is encouraged through the practical computer workshops which allow students to explore and work through the topic material at their own pace. Through the practical workshops, students are building up confidence in their skills and becoming more digitally fluent.

Teaching Method 1 - Videos
Description: Short videos covering each topic are provided on the VLE
Attendance Recorded: No
Notes: These short online videos cover both the underlying theory behind each statistical test, an example of that test in action and then a worked-through example in the statistical software.

Unscheduled Directed Student Hours (time spent away from the timetabled sessions but directed by the teaching staff): 20

Teaching Method 2 - Practical
Description: Tutorial workshops to supplement learning.
Attendance Recorded: Yes
Notes: After each topic is covered in the online videos, a guided worksheet for the corresponding workshop will be released. Students are expected to begin reading and working through the exercises on the worksheet in preparation for the workshop. This work is done on computer which allows for students to have flexibility about where and when the work is done and the worksheets are designed to enable this option. At the end of the workshops are a number of do-it-yourself questions where students have to put their knowledge into practice. The following week, the DIY question answers are released as a walk-through video.
Unscheduled Directed Student Hours (time spent away from the timetabled sessions but directed by the teaching staff): 66

Additional drop-in sessions for 1:1 general and poster assessment support are available as well throughout the course.

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for teaching may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.
Assessment Strategy
The assessment strategy was devised with Curriculum 2021 Hallmarks and Attributes in mind. The assessments detailed below are examples of authentic assessment, such as presenting scientific research at a poster event and research-connected teaching, such as the real-world scientific datasets utilised for the poster assessment. Furthermore, the assessments below build confidence and digital literacy skills in our Masters students to enhance their employability following the course.

Assessment 1 is an SAQ exam. This exam takes place at the end of the course during the January exam period. There is a resit opportunity available for the exam, which is capped at 50%.

Assessment 2: Poster presentation. This will be an individual poster assignment which will be take place at a conference style event (alongside LIFE223 students) in week 12. There is a resit opportunity available for the poster, which is capped at 50%.

Assessment 3: One supplementary pieces of analysis and report writing. In line with the new assessment strategy, this assessment takes place in cycle 1 of the course. This assessment builds upon the material covered in computer tutorials. There is a resit opportunity for the coursework component, which is capped at 50%.

Feedback will be provided to all assessments. Pass mark for all assessments is 50%. Standard UoL penalty applies for late submission.

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for assessment may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.
