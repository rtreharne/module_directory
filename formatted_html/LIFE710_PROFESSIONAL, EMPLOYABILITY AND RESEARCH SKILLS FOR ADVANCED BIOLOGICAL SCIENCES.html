Blurb
Overview
This module comprises a wide range of activities designed to help postgraduate science students develop skills that make them resourceful, responsible and adaptable graduates who are attractive to employers.

The activities include the opportunities to further develop studentsâ communication, teamwork, interpersonal, problem solving, research and analytical, planning and organizing, technology, and lifelong learning skills. The teaching delivered on this module supports studentsâ learning on other modules by allowing students to apply their knowledge and skills they will need for their research project.

Students are introduced to academic integrity, intellectual property rights, information retrieval & databases and referencing tools to set a strong foundation for students to start off their research projects.

Students demonstrate these skills by submitting a project summary that reflects their ability to perform critical evaluation of the authenticity of a literature review, understand and apply knowledge of the research integrity in planning and executing their research and health and safety.

Interactive workshops are delivered on writing grant proposals and reviewing articles using critical thinking and evaluation, which they are able to demonstrate through the assignment on other research modules.

To help advance their communication skills to varied audiences, students are engaged in public activities where they create posters on the life sciences research of the institute and present to local school pupils aged 14-16 years.

The students work in groups which allows them to enhance their team-working skills. In order to enhance students digital fluency students are introduced to different digital platforms and are expected to choose one and create an animated video on their research project.

Workshops are delivered to support students for job applications through the CV clinics and mock interviews.
Educational Aims
To develop and acquire a range of personal and interpersonal skills relating to effective self-management and working relationships with others;

To develop and acquire an enhanced ability to communicate effectively in academic and professional settings;

To develop and acquire skills and knowledge required for enhanced employability;

To develop and acquire a range of generic skills that will help with research planning and delivery to a professional standard.
Marketing Module Synopsis
This module comprises a wide range of activities designed to help postgraduate science students develop skills that make them resourceful, responsible and adaptable graduates who are attractive to employers.

This module prepares students for enhanced communication, teamwork, interpersonal, problem solving, research and analytical, planning and organizing, technology, and lifelong learning skills.

Students will acquire knowledge in academic integrity, information retrieval, referencing tools, grant proposal writing and critical thinking; the tools that help to set up students for the start of their research project.

Students will also develop communication skills to a varied audience by engaging in public activities whilst working in groups to enhance their team-working skills. To make students digitally confident, they will be introduced to different digital platforms and will utilize these to promote their online presence, thus enhancing their employability prospects.

The module will prepare students for their job application process through CV clinics and mock interviews.
Outline Syllabus
1. Studying at Masters Level Developing work and study skills at M level; Learning/Reflective Logs, and Record of Skills using GoogleDocs; Communication and Team-Working Skills

2. Health and Safety Understanding and applying University and Departmental safety requirements. Risk assessments, handling hazardous chemicals, COSSH, first aid. Personal responsibility. (requires proven attendance at all University/Department safety sessions for new research members).

3. Information Retrieval and Databases/Bibliographic databases for Informatics. Students attend a session given by the Science Librarian where they consider methods of accessing a variety of on-line and web-based scientific and bibliographic databases.

4. Endnote Exercise in use of Endnote

5. Writing a research grant proposal

6. Critical reading and writing

7. Critical thinking and reflection

8. Preparing a poster

9. Employability and Educational Opportunities in Biological Sciences Talk from the Careers Service and workshop on career opportunities

10. Preparing a CV and Letter of Application; applying for a PhD/job

11. Preparing for a viva/interview

12. Reviewing scientific literature

13. Writing a scientific paper

14. Preparing and delivering a presentation

15. Science on the Read/Write Web Recent years have seen scientists increasingly engage with their peers and the public via the World-Wide Web. This workshop will look at some of the reasons for doing this (communication and advocacy, data sharing and curation, reduction in travel costs and impact) and try to identify good practice that participants can translate into action via their own blogs, wikis, etc. We will also be looking at issues associated with collaborative authoring and, in particular, synchronous editing of documents.
Teaching and Learning Methods
The teaching and learning strategy has been guided by the Liverpool Curriculum Framework attributes and hallmarks, particularly digital fluency, global citizenship and research connected teaching. This strategy will also help develop studentâs employability prospects.

Teaching Method 1 -Asynchronous lecture recording to introduce the topic, pre-lecture reading material and assessments.
Teaching Method 2 - Synchronous /F2F Interactive workshops including the formative feedback sessions prior to final submission of assessments
Teaching Method 3 -Group project workshops and feedback
Teaching Method 4 - Self directed study. The self-study hours will include engaging with asynchronous learning resources and reading list posted by the academic. viewing of online resources, exploring and practicing digital platforms.

Liverpool Curriculum Framework addressed:

Research connected teaching: The lectures and workshops delivered on the module prepare students with advanced research skills and support them with their research projects

Active learning: The interactive workshops on grant proposal and critical thinking and the group work on communication skills provide them real world scenarios that promotes active learning

Authentic assessments: The assessments mirror the real-world scenarios that allow students to see the direct application of knowledge and skills.

Digital fluency: The module delivers teaching on the effective use of digital platforms to enhance communication skills and to reach to the global audience through online media.

"Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for teaching may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given."
Assessment Strategy
Assessment strategy has been guided by the Liverpool Curriculum Framework attributes and hallmarks, particularly authentic assessments. Students will have to complete three tasks: (1) preparing and delivering a poster (in groups); (2) a digital case study; (3) a project summary (that includes sections on Health and Safety, Referencing, Research integrity and IP). These assignments will relate to their project work and resources and assessments take place ahead of the project work. These are authentic assessments and will build the students' self-confidence, understanding of research philosophy and digital fluency. These tasks mimic those that students may expect to undertake in graduate-level positions.

Reassessment strategy
There is a re-assessment opportunity that take the form of assignments of the types described above. It will take place in the normal reassessment window and be capped at 50%.

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for assessment may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given
Assessment 1:
Preparing 3 minute animated video on the research projects and digital profile.

Assessment 2:
Group work consisting of preparing and presenting poster.

Assessment 3:
Project summary with sections on critical evaluation of literature, referencing, academic integrity and health and safety review.
