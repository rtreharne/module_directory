Blurb
Overview
This is a 30-credit module that gives direct authentic training in the development and planning of a novel empirical research project. The teaching approach relies upon research-led and enquiry-based learning with authentic assessments in the form of a preliminary project proposal together with continual assessment of the student’s performance (Hallmarks of the Liverpool Curriculum Framework).
This module along with the Year 2 research project module (LIFE736 Advanced Biological Sciences Research Project 2) is designed to allow for part time study.
The project will be closely associated with their chosen pathway and under the direct supervision of a project supervisor. Each pathway will offer a broad range of projects, which provide the opportunity for students to select research topics that offer a range of integrated training in key aspects of modern biological science including bioinformatics, animal studies, cell and tissue culture, systematic reviews and meta-analyses, programming, understanding and interpreting omic data, and computer modelling. Projects will offer diverse experiences in research techniques, governance, project management and analysis in an area of Biological Science.
The module is continuously assessed, with opportunities for feedback on all elements before final submission. Online resources will support self-directed learning. Students will be given initial training in relevant research techniques required for the project, but will develop the ability to work independently as the project progresses.
This approach is consistent with the Liverpool Hallmarks of research-connected teaching and active learning as set out in the Liverpool Curriculum Framework.
Educational Aims
Students will be given the opportunity to:
• work in a guided but independent fashion on an area of research related to the chosen pathway, making use of the knowledge and skills acquired elsewhere in the programme
• study a single topic in substantial depth
Marketing Module Synopsis
The MRes in Advanced Biological Science provides students with high level research training within the Institute of Systems, Molecular and Integrative Medicine (ISMIB). Our aim is to develop research knowledge, skills and attributes including confidence and expertise, to contribute to the pursuit of further study or undertake roles in academia, or in the health sector, industry, education or other service sectors. The programme is suitable for bioscience graduates, intercalating medical or veterinary students, and science graduates who are interested in training in advanced biological sciences. Students will become full members of a research group and take advantage of first-class facilities and an active research environment.
This is a 30-credit module that gives direct authentic experience in developing and planning a novel empirical research project. The module represents the first part of a two-part research project offered to students who are studying a part time MRes in Advanced Biological Sciences. Typically, students will work on a dedicated research project. The teaching approach relies upon research-led and enquiry-based learning with authentic assessments in the form of scientific reports, a scientific presentation and continual assessment of the student’s performance (Hallmarks of the Liverpool Curriculum Framework).
Outline Syllabus
Students will normally choose their project from a predefined list of available projects in each pathway. The Preliminary Report component of the module provides an important opportunity for authentic learning through the development of a range of key skills including critical review of relevant literature, experimental design, planning and time management. In addition, this exercise also provides a valuable opportunity for formative training in scientific writing, document and figure formatting and correct referencing techniques, all skills that are subsequently required for preparation of the final project report. All projects must be designed to follow an evidence-based empirical approach to address a novel area of research, which is aligned to the pathway selected by the student.

In addition to the support provided by their supervisor, students will receive hands on training in required experimental skills, data analysis and interpretation from other members of the research group and technical staff within their host department and Institute. Support for skills development will be available throughout the project, providing a supportive incremental formative approach to project planning, problem solving, data interpretation and reasoned adaptability.

Working together with the supervisor, students will develop important transferable skills such as, working within a team, problem solving and adaptability, critical thinking, and scientific creativity.

Students will also have opportunities to develop effective communication skills, though authentic learning experiences in which they are required to present their findings in oral, digital and written formats. These, components of the module help to build self-confidence as well as presentation and related digital skills.
Teaching and Learning Methods
Learning and teaching on this module is designed to ensure that teaching is informed and refreshed by research and best evidence (‘Research-connected teaching’, Liverpool Curriculum Framework). Teaching and supervision will be undertaken by researchers in their specialist area. Students can expect one formal supervision meeting per month, alongside continual supervision within the lab setting. The teaching method and content is based on a strong knowledge foundation and theoretical application of knowledge (Research connected teaching, Liverpool Curriculum Framework). Students are expected to spend most of their time critically reviewing the literature, planning and conducting their research, analysing and interpreting results in light of the current literature, and preparing communications i.e. preliminary report. Regular meetings with the project supervisor(s) are required to ensure students are properly guided and provided with constructive feedback on progress and assignments. Digital resources including videos, workshops and recommended literature also support student learning. The module builds on their understanding of research processes, experimental design, conducting and analysing an empirical research project, research governance and scientific communications via the research project. Laboratory teaching will be undertaken by an experienced member of staff. Once competent, students will undertake the laboratory work under supervision and finally independently or as part of a larger group.

The teaching delivery is based on the research supervisor model used for postgraduate research degrees, active learning and supporting confidence (Liverpool Curriculum Framework). Supervisors will guide the students through the research, planning and safety assessment, in a research report format (Digital fluency, Liverpool Curriculum Framework). The underpinning philosophy for this approach to delivery is independent learning that engages and challenges students in an active learning process (Liverpool Curriculum Framework). The Departmental research environment and informal teaching sessions - introduce new learning, reinforcing prior learning around research and methodologies and guide the student through their learning process.

Students on this programme come from a wide background. Discussion between students and research colleagues affords the students a rich opportunity to experience and understand cultures other than their own. The formation of this multinational community fosters the values of respect and equality amongst the students and improves their internationalised perspective (Global citizenship, Liverpool Curriculum Framework).

This teaching and learning strategy, is tailored to the specific needs of the student, and will help them to further develop their specialist research knowledge and understanding, critical thinking skills and research skills. This approach is consistent with the Liverpool Hallmarks of research-connected teaching and active learning as set out in the Liverpool Curriculum Framework.

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for teaching and learning may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.
Assessment Strategy
All students work on a single dedicated research project, the content of which is primarily directed by the academic supervisor of the project.
The module is assessed by submission of a preliminary project report and the supervisor’s assessment of the students work throughout the project. A summative preliminary report in Semester one, encourages students to demonstrate understanding of the background and rationale of the project, whilst also providing clear aims and a project plan. Written assessments are submitted and marked online for all students including detailed feedback from the supervisor on a draft copy prior to the final submission. For students who have chosen an online project all of these assignments can be completed in an online environment.

The preliminary report will not be marked anonymously as it will need to be marked by the supervisor, an expert on the research topic and so the student will be easily identified. However, all proposals will be independently second marked by someone who hasn’t been involved in the delivery of the project. The reports reflect real-life scenarios (hallmarks of the Liverpool Curriculum Framework). The preliminary report being an outline proposal which would be common in requesting funding for a pilot study and would include a hypothesis, supporting evidence aimed to encourage critical analysis of the literature, development of a feasible project idea and understanding.

Students will be offered a re-assessment opportunity in failed assessments in accordance with the Code of Practice on Assessment Appendix C.
Students resitting the preliminary report will be allowed to re-submit on their project.
Students resitting the module will be required to develop a completely new project with a new supervisor.
Resit opportunities are usually within 12 months of the failed assessment, and will be capped at the pass mark (50%) unless taken as a first sitting.

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for assessment may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given

The Departmental research environment and informal teaching sessions - introduce new learning, reinforcing prior learning around research and methodologies and guide the student through their learning process.

Students on this programme come from a wide background. The formation of this multinational community fosters the values of respect and equality amongst the students and improves their internationalised perspective (Global citizenship, Liverpool Curriculum Framework).

This teaching and learning strategy, is tailored to the specific needs of the student, and will help them to further develop their specialist research knowledge and understanding, critical thinking skills and research skills. Marketing Module Synopsis
The MRes in Advanced Biological Science provides students with high level research training within the Institute of Systems, Molecular and Integrative Medicine (ISMIB). Our aim is to develop research knowledge, skills and attributes including confidence and expertise, to contribute to the pursuit of further study or undertake roles in academia, or in the health sector, industry, education or other service sectors. The programme is suitable for bioscience graduates, intercalating medical or veterinary students, and science graduates who are interested in training in advanced biological sciences. Students will become full members of a research group and take advantage of first-class facilities and an active research environment.
This is a 30-credit module that gives direct authentic experience in developing and planning a novel empirical research project. The module represents the first part of a two-part research project offered to students who are studying a part time MRes in Advanced Biological Sciences. Outline Syllabus
Students will normally choose their project from a predefined list of available projects in each pathway. The Preliminary Report component of the module provides an important opportunity for authentic learning through the development of a range of key skills including critical review of relevant literature, experimental design, planning and time management. In addition, this exercise also provides a valuable opportunity for formative training in scientific writing, document and figure formatting and correct referencing techniques, all skills that are subsequently required for preparation of the final project report. All projects must be designed to follow an evidence-based empirical approach to address a novel area of research, which is aligned to the pathway selected by the student.

In addition to the support provided by their supervisor, students will receive hands on training in required experimental skills, data analysis and interpretation from other members of the research group and technical staff within their host department and Institute. Support for skills development will be available throughout the project, providing a supportive incremental formative approach to project planning, problem solving, data interpretation and reasoned adaptability.

Working together with the supervisor, students will develop important transferable skills such as, working within a team, problem solving and adaptability, critical thinking, and scientific creativity.

Students will also have opportunities to develop effective communication skills, though authentic learning experiences in which they are required to present their findings in oral, digital and written formats. These, components of the module help to build self-confidence as well as presentation and related digital skills.

Regular meetings with the project supervisor(s) are required to ensure students are properly guided and provided with constructive feedback on progress and assignments. Digital resources including videos, workshops and recommended literature also support student learning. The module builds on their understanding of research processes, experimental design, conducting and analysing an empirical research project, research governance and scientific communications via the research project. Laboratory teaching will be undertaken by an experienced member of staff. Once competent, students will undertake the laboratory work under supervision and finally independently or as part of a larger group.
