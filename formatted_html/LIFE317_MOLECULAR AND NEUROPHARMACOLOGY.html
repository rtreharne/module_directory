Rationale
Graeme Sills is leaving University of Liverpool at the end of Sept 2018.
Blurb
Overview
​ The aim of this module is to  provide a contemporary review of drug treatment for the most common disorders of the brain, focusing on pathophysiology, receptors and ion channels as drug targets, and the mechanisms of action of key classes of neuropharmacological agents The module will be assessed by both continuous assessment and by a final examination.
Educational Aims
​ To provide a contemporary review of drug treatment for the most common disorders of the brain, focusing on pathophysiology, receptors and ion channels as drug targets, and the mechanisms of action of key classes of neuropharmacological agents  
Marketing Module Synopsis
​ The aim of this module is to  provide a contemporary review of drug treatment for the most common disorders of the brain, focusing on pathophysiology, receptors and ion channels as drug targets, and the mechanisms of action of key classes of neuropharmacological agents The module will be assessed by both continuous assessment and by a final examination.
Outline Syllabus
​Neurotransmission in the nervous system, focusing on glutamate and GABA as neurotransmitters, treatments for depression, anxiety etc. G-protein coupled receptors, structure activity relationships, their role in adrenergic neurotransmission, pharmacological manipulation of GPCRs Epilepsy, antiepileptic drug development, pharmacology of antiepileptic drugs, drug resistance in epilepsy Neurodegenerative disorders, pathophysiology and drug treatment of Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease, latest developments in neuroimaging of the brain.
Teaching and Learning Methods
Teaching Method 1 -asynchronous (pre-recorded) Lecture
Description: Lectures to introduce key topics

Teaching Method 2 - synchronous (live) Tutorial
Description: synchronous (live)

Learning and teaching in this module are designed and delivered by research-active scientists to ensure that teaching is informed by the latest research and best evidence (‘Research-connected teaching’, Liverpool Curriculum Framework).

Delivery methods may include PBL, flipped classrooms, polling, and discussions based on pre-lecture reading or recordings etc. These engage and challenge students in an active learning process, and coupled with independent learning, will build self-confidence (Liverpool Curriculum Framework.

Lecture tutorial content, reading lists, and discussion between students and staff give the students an opportunity to experience and understand cultures other than their own. The formation of this inclusive approach fosters the values of respect and equality amongst the students and improves their global perspective (Global citizenship and Inclusivity, Liverpool Curriculum Framework).

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for teaching may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.
Assessment Strategy
Assessment 1 is a written piece of coursework comprising interpretation of a classic neuropharmacology paper;
Assessment 2 is an essay style assessment comprising of essay style questions.

The assessments reflect real-life and career-based scenarios (Authentic assessment hallmark of the Liverpool Curriculum Framework).

Specifically, these assessments enhance digital fluency in our students, a skill highly sought after by graduate employers. Additionally, we are creating confident graduates who will excel in their field. These authentic assessments are research-connected and the skills for completion are taught through active learning

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for assessment may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.

Blurb
Overview
​ The aim of this module is to  provide a contemporary review of drug treatment for the most common disorders of the brain, focusing on pathophysiology, receptors and ion channels as drug targets, and the mechanisms of action of key classes of neuropharmacological agents The module will be assessed by both continuous assessment and by a final examination.
Educational Aims
​ To provide a contemporary review of drug treatment for the most common disorders of the brain, focusing on pathophysiology, receptors and ion channels as drug targets, and the mechanisms of action of key classes of neuropharmacological agents  
Marketing Module Synopsis
​ The aim of this module is to  provide a contemporary review of drug treatment for the most common disorders of the brain, focusing on pathophysiology, receptors and ion channels as drug targets, and the mechanisms of action of key classes of neuropharmacological agents The module will be assessed by both continuous assessment and by a final examination.
Outline Syllabus
​Neurotransmission in the nervous system, focusing on glutamate and GABA as neurotransmitters, treatments for depression, anxiety etc. G-protein coupled receptors, structure activity relationships, their role in adrenergic neurotransmission, pharmacological manipulation of GPCRs Epilepsy, antiepileptic drug development, pharmacology of antiepileptic drugs, drug resistance in epilepsy Neurodegenerative disorders, pathophysiology and drug treatment of Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease, latest developments in neuroimaging of the brain.
Teaching and Learning Methods
Teaching Method 1 -asynchronous (pre-recorded) Lecture
Description: Lectures to introduce key topics

Teaching Method 2 - synchronous (live) Tutorial
Description: synchronous (live)

Learning and teaching in this module are designed and delivered by research-active scientists to ensure that teaching is informed by the latest research and best evidence (‘Research-connected teaching’, Liverpool Curriculum Framework).

Delivery methods may include PBL, flipped classrooms, polling, and discussions based on pre-lecture reading or recordings etc. These engage and challenge students in an active learning process, and coupled with independent learning, will build self-confidence (Liverpool Curriculum Framework.

Lecture tutorial content, reading lists, and discussion between students and staff give the students an opportunity to experience and understand cultures other than their own. The formation of this inclusive approach fosters the values of respect and equality amongst the students and improves their global perspective (Global citizenship and Inclusivity, Liverpool Curriculum Framework).

Students will be informed that, in exceptional circumstances (e.g. global pandemics), plans for teaching may be subject to change. Contact will be made via the VLE and an e-mail will be sent to each of the students informing them of any changes that prove to be necessary. The Health and Life Sciences FAQSC will be informed in advance of any such changes, so that approval can be given.
Assessment Strategy
Assessment 1 is a written piece of coursework comprising interpretation of a classic neuropharmacology paper;
Assessment 2 is an essay style assessment comprising of essay style questions.

The assessments reflect real-life and career-based scenarios (Authentic assessment hallmark of the Liverpool Curriculum Framework).

Specifically, these assessments enhance digital fluency in our students, a skill highly sought after by graduate employers. Additionally, we are creating confident graduates who will excel in their field. These authentic assessments are research-connected and the skills for completion are taught through active learning

Learning Outcomes
 ReferenceOutcome 
 LO1 Critically evaluate the central role of excitatory and inhibitory neurotransmission in the nervous system in health and disease​
 LO2 ​Identify major classes of drug targets in the nervous system and explain how those targets interact with intracellular effector mechanisms​
 LO3 ​Analyse the characteristic features of epilepsy, the range of drugs available for its treatment, and how those drugs interact with specific targets in the brain​
 LO4 ​Evaluate current hypotheses to explain cell loss in neurodegenerative diseases and explain how drugs can arrest the symptoms of those disorders and how neuroimaging is helping to improve understanding of the underlying pathology ​
